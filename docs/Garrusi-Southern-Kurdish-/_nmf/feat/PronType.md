In Naga-Suansu, `PronType` is a feature of pronouns ([PRON]()), determiners ([DET]()), and pronominal adverbs ([ADV]()).

### <a name="Prs">`Prs`</a>: personal pronoun

The examples below illustrate Naga-Suansu independent personal pronouns. Dual forms are not attested in the corpus.


#### Examples

* _a, hai, nahn, na, ba, bu_ "I, we, you (sing.), you (plur.), he/she/it, they"

### <a name="Int">`Int`</a>: interrogative pronoun, determiner or adverb

#### Examples

* _thuza_ "who"

    * _<b>Thuza</b> gali tha ma la khaliuh nan window di rakhie matam da?_ "**Who** didn't wake up when the thieves smashed the window?"

* _mwe_ "what/which", _mwethe_ "why"

    * _nahn <b>mwe</b> tha?_ "What did you see?"

    * _Bai nan rungthai <b>mwethe</b> da ba ashu re ngam ma ganan laia la re._ "She explained **why** she couldn't come yesterday."

* _gare_ "how" (in _gare lehnda_ "which way")

    * _Mercy nan leneo pha la di hai the di <b>gare lehn da</b> tharahn the laila re katamthai ganan laile._ "Mercy is teaching the girls how to prepare this meat."

### <a name="Dem">`Dem`</a>: demonstrative pronoun, determiner or adverb

Demonstratives are distinguished by [Deixis]().

#### Examples

* _hadi, didi_ "this one, that one"


    * _<b>hadi</b> lua da!_ "Take this!"

    * _<b>didi</b> lua da!_ "Take that!"



* _hai, tye_ "this, that"

    * _<b>hai</b> lairakdi lua da!_ "Take this book!"

    * _<b>tye</b> lairakdi lua da!_ "Take that book!"


* _hano, dino_ "here, there"

    * _ba <b>hano</b> thathe rahn_ "She/He will work here."

    * _ba <b>dino</b> thathe rahn_ "She/He will work there."


### <a name="Tot">`Tot`</a>: total (collective) pronoun or determiner

#### Examples

* _mazohn_ "everybody / every"

    * _Jasonva baswua <b>mazohn</b> party nahn re rahn._ "**All** Jason's friends will come to the party."

### <a name="Ind">`Ind`</a>: indefinite pronoun or determiner

#### Examples

* _neo_ "someone", _chatha_ "others"

    * _Asza hai thungthe, he, phuirai ska, rate ragu <b>chatha</b> theza re thaiate._ "Today we have seen monkeys, birds, a snake, and **other** animals."

* _za_ "some"

    * _ba jaa <b>za</b> runge._ "She/He said **some** words."
