In Khoekhoe, `Case` is a feature of nominals: [nouns](naq-pos/NOUN), [proper nouns](naq-pos/PROPN), and [pronouns](naq-pos/PRON). It is also an inflectional feature of other parts of speech ([adjectives](naq-pos/ADJ), [determiners](naq-pos/DET)) that agree with nominals when they follow them, and of [numerals](naq-pos/NUM) that function as nominals (e.g., _ǀguis ge ge kō_ "one looked"). Also some nominal [adverbs](naq-pos/ADV) (like _tsîn(a)_ "also", _on(a)_ "too") and the nominalizer _s_ (as [particle](naq-pos/PART) or [subordinate conjunction](naq-pos/SCONJ)) have the [Case](naq-feat/Case) feature (only `Nom` and `Acc`) because they take the case of the whole noun phrase.


### <a name="Nom">`Nom`</a>: direct

Used for subject person-gender-number clitics, clause subjects in the place of person-gender-number clitics, and with most postpositions.

#### Examples

* _<b>Petrub</b> ge <b>oms<b> ǃnâ go ǂgâ._ "**Peter** entered into the **house**."

* _O<b>b</b> ge ǁnâuǃgâǃkhaiba sida ǃoa nē ǁhōba go ao._ "Then the **court** tossed his matter to us."

* _<b>Sida tsîn</b> ge a ǀū._ "**We also** don't know."

### <a name="Acc">`Acc`</a>: oblique

Used for direct and indirect objects, clause subjects in clauses with separate subject person-gender-number clitic, and with some postpositions. The oblique case is usually marked with the suffix _-a_, except for dedicated pronominal objects.

#### Examples

* _ǁîs ge <b>ǁîra</b> <b>ǀaena</b> ra mā._ "She bundles **firewood** for **them**."

* _ǁGawaǁae <b>te</b> re._ "Excuse **me**."

* _Ob ge <b>ǁnâuǃgâǃkhaiba</b> <b>sida</b> ǃoa nē <b>ǁhōba</b> go ao_ "Then the **court** tossed this **matter** to **us**."

* _<b>ǁAmaxūǁgâuga</b> ǃoa ta nî ī
…._ "I got to the **store**…"

### <a name="Voc">`Voc`</a>: vocative

The vocative case is a special form of nouns used to address someone. It is marked with the _-e_ suffix.

#### Examples

* _<b>ǃGôahesatse</b>, nēpa i ge ǃgomsiba hâ._ "**Sir**, there is a difficulty."

* _<b>Abotse</b>, ǀûba te!_ "**God**, forgive me!"
