In Khoekhoe, `Number` is a feature of nominals: [nouns](naq-pos/NOUN), [proper nouns](naq-pos/PROPN), and [pronouns](naq-pos/PRON). It is also an inflectional feature of other parts of speech ([adjectives](naq-pos/ADJ), [determiners](naq-pos/ADV)), and [adverbs](naq-pos/DET)) that mark agreement with nominals when they follow them, and of [numerals](naq-pos/NUM) when they function as nominals (e.g., _ǀguis ge ge kō_ "one looked").

### <a name="Sing">`Sing`</a>: singular number

A singular noun denotes one person, animal or thing.

#### Examples

* _khoeb_ "a man"

* _gomas_ "a cow"

* _xū-i_ "a thing"

* _tita_ "I", _sats_ "you (masc.)", _sas_ "you (fem.)", _ǁîb_ "he" _ǁîs_ "she", _ǁî-i_ "it"

### <a name="Plur">`Plur`</a>: plural number

A plural noun denotes several (more than two) persons, animals or things.

#### Examples

* _khoegu_ "men"

* _gomadi_ "cows"

* _xūn_ "things"

* _sige_ "we (masc. excl.)", _sise_ "we (fem. excl.)", _sida_ "we (neut. excl.)",
  _sage_ "we (masc. incl.)", _sase_ "we (fem. incl.)", _sada_ "we (neut. incl.)",
  _sago_ "you (masc.)", _saso_ "you (fem.)", _sadu_ "you (neut.)",
  _ǁîgu_ "they (masc.)", _ǁîdi_ "they (fem.)", _ǁîn_ "they (neut.)"

### <a name="Dual">`Dual`</a>: dual number

A dual noun denotes two persons, animals or things.

#### Examples

* _khoekha_ "two men"

* _gomara_ "two cows"

* _xūra_ "two things"

* _sikhom_ "we two (masc. excl.)", _sim_ "we two (fem./neut. excl.)",
  _sakhom_ "we two (masc. excl.)", _sam_ "we two (fem./neut. excl.)",
  _sakho_ "you two (masc.)", _saro_ "you two (fem./neut.)",
  _ǁîkha_ "they two (masc.)", _ǁîra_ "they two (fem./neut.)"
