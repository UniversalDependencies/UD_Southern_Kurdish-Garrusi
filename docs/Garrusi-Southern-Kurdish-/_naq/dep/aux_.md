An auxiliary of a clause is a function word associated with a verbal predicate that expresses categories such as tense, mood, aspect, voice or evidentiality. Most auxiliaries in Khoekhoe are nonverbal, but there are several exceptions (_sī, mâ_).

~~~ sdparse
Saras ge ariba ra ǃao . \n Sara DECL dog.OBL IPFV afraid .
aux(ǃao, ge)
aux(ǃao, ra)
aux(afraid, DECL)
aux(afraid, IPFV)
~~~

~~~ sdparse
Natalies ge sa koffie-e sī ra ū . \n Natalie DECL your coffee come IPFV take .
aux(ū, ge)
aux(ū, sī)
aux(ū, ra)
aux(take, DECL)
aux(take, come)
aux(take, IPFV)
~~~
