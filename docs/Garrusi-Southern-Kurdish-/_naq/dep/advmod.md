An adverbial modifier of a word is a (non-clausal) [adverb](naq-pos/ADV) or adverbial phrase that serves to modify a predicate, a modifier word, or a nominal.

~~~ sdparse
Sida ge nētsē ama gawuxa-ai tsēsa ūhâ . \n We DECL today really cloudy day have .
advmod(ūhâ, nētsē)
advmod(gawuxa-ai, ama)
advmod(have, today)
advmod(cloudy, really)
~~~

~~~ sdparse
Mâti ts ǀon hâ ? \n How you be_called PFV ?
advmod(ǀon, Mâti)
advmod(be_called, How)
~~~

~~~ sdparse
Sida tsîn ge a ǀū . \n We also DECL STAT.PRS not_know .
advmod(Sida, tsîn)
advmod(We, also)
~~~

~~~ sdparse
O dī ts nî ? \n Then do you will ?
advmod(dī, O)
advmod(do, Then)
~~~

Note that negative particle _tama_ is also annotated with the `advmod` relation.

~~~ sdparse
ǁÎba nē daoba u hā tama hâ ? \n He this way along come not PFV ?
advmod(hā, tama)
advmod(come, not)
~~~
