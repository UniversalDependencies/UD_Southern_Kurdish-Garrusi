### Definition

Punctuation marks are non-alphabetical characters and character groups used to delimit linguistic units in printed text. They can consist of a sequence of symbols, such as a question mark followed by an exclamation mark (?!), an exclamation mark followed by two full stops (!..) and ellipsis (…). 

### Corresponding language-specific part-of-speech tags

F: Punctuation marks

### Examples

Period: _<b>.</b>_
Comma: _<b>,</b>_
Parentheses: _<b>(</b>_, _<b>)</b>_
